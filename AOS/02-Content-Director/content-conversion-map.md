# Content Conversion Map

What a trigger typically converts into. A guide, not a rule: use
judgment on any given trigger, but don't skip the check.

| Trigger | LinkedIn Post | Newsletter | Website Update (Insights) | Downloadable Resource | Consulting Offer |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Regulatory change (new act, standard, guidance) | Yes, if there's a specific practitioner take, not a summary | Yes, longer treatment | Yes, if it changes an existing Insights piece's accuracy | Sometimes, if it's substantial enough to need a Framework Guide | Yes, flag who it creates urgency for |
| Client question (recurring) | Yes, if more than one client has asked it | Sometimes | Yes, as a Governance Note | Sometimes, if the answer is reusable (a Checklist or Playbook) | Rarely direct, but reveals a services gap |
| LinkedIn discussion / market chatter | Yes, if AI for U&I has a distinct point of view | Rarely | Rarely | No | Rarely |
| Engagement pattern (something seen repeatedly across advisory work) | Yes, as a Point of View piece | Yes | Yes, this is the strongest source for original Insights | Yes, if the pattern is common enough to templatise | Indirectly, builds authority that supports future proposals |
| Product launch or update | Yes | Yes | Yes, update the Resources listing | The product itself | Sometimes, bundled with an advisory offer |

## Notes

- A regulatory change should never produce *only* a LinkedIn post
  summarising the text. If there's no distinct practitioner take,
  it's not worth publishing yet, wait until there is one.
- The strongest original content comes from real engagement patterns,
  not from reacting to the news cycle. Weight `content-brief-template.md`
  toward the "Engagement pattern" row when possible.
