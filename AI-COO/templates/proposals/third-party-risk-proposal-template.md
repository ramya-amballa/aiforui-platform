## Understanding the Challenge

{{CLIENT_NAME}}'s third-party risk programme is {{SPECIFIC_CONTEXT,
e.g. "strong at onboarding, weak at everything after," "unclear on who
owns a vendor relationship once onboarding ends," "not proportional to
vendor criticality"}}. The risk that actually materialises usually
lives after signature, not at onboarding: a subprocessor changes, a
data flow expands, a control lapses, and nothing triggers review
unless someone is assigned to look for it.

## Proposed Approach

Run through OPERA, adapted to the full vendor lifecycle:

- **Opportunity** — segment vendors by criticality and data access,
  rather than one assessment for every vendor
- **People** — assign internal ownership for each lifecycle stage,
  including contract, monitoring and exit
- **Evaluation** — define assessment criteria proportional to that
  segmentation: security, resilience, data handling, concentration
  risk
- **Response** — build a monitoring cadence that catches drift between
  contracted and actual vendor practice
- **Assurance** — an evidence trail proportional to vendor criticality,
  not a single annual questionnaire
