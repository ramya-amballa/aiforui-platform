## Understanding the Challenge

{{CLIENT_NAME}} needs to operationalise the EU AI Act's risk-tiered
obligations for {{SPECIFIC_CONTEXT, e.g. "a specific high-risk AI
system," "an AI inventory across multiple business units," "conformity
assessment preparation"}}. The Act is rarely the hard part to
understand; it is translating risk-tier obligations into ownership,
evidence and an operating rhythm that a regulator or notified body can
actually examine.

## Proposed Approach

Run through OPERA, adapted to EU AI Act risk tiering:

- **Opportunity** — catalogue AI systems and classify them against the
  Act's risk tiers before assessing anything else
- **People** — assign a named owner per AI system and per governance
  decision, matching the Act's accountability expectations
- **Evaluation** — assess each system's obligations by tier: risk
  management, data governance, technical documentation, human
  oversight
- **Response** — build the decision and approval structure the Act
  requires for high-risk systems specifically
- **Assurance** — the technical documentation and evidence trail a
  conformity assessment will require
