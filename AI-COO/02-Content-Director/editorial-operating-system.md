# Content Director — Editorial Operating System

## Mission

Convert regulatory changes, client questions and market signals into
content that does one of five specific jobs. No content gets
published without a clear answer to "which objective does this
serve, and how will we know it worked."

## The Five Objectives

| Objective | What success looks like | Primary formats |
|---|---|---|
| Generate consulting leads | A reader starts a conversation or downloads the Executive Capability Overview | LinkedIn post with a specific, narrow claim; newsletter issue; Insights piece |
| Sell products | A reader buys or requests a Toolkit, Checklist, Course or Workshop | Product-launch LinkedIn post; newsletter feature; landing page copy |
| Build authority | Shares, saves, inbound recognition, being cited or quoted elsewhere | Original-thinking LinkedIn post; longer newsletter essay; Point of View Insights piece |
| Increase newsletter subscribers | Subscriber growth attributable to a specific piece | LinkedIn post with a clear subscribe call-to-action; guest appearances; lead magnets |
| Support website SEO | Organic traffic and ranking for governance-relevant search terms | Insights and Resources pages, written for the search terms a CISO or board member would actually use |

Every piece of content should be evaluated against this table before
it is drafted, not after.

## From Trigger to Content

1. A trigger arrives: a regulatory change (`05-Market-Intelligence`), a
   client question (`04-Sales-Director` or `06-CRM`), a LinkedIn
   discussion, or a pattern noticed across several of these.
2. Check `content-conversion-map.md` for which output formats and
   objectives this kind of trigger typically fits.
3. Pick the objective(s) this specific trigger actually serves, don't
   default to "all five."
4. Fill in `content-brief-template.md`.
5. Draft. If the piece reveals a larger product opportunity, flag it to
   `03-Product-Manager`. If it reveals a specific consulting angle for
   a specific company, flag it to `08-Revenue-Hunter` and
   `04-Sales-Director`.
6. Log it in `content-calendar-template.md` with its objective and, once
   published, its actual result.

## Daily Workflow

1. Pull today's entries from `05-Market-Intelligence/regulatory-log.json`
   marked substantive.
2. Pull any client questions logged in
   `06-CRM/company-intelligence.json` or flagged by `04-Sales-Director`
   this week.
3. For each, run the trigger-to-content process above.
4. Update `content-calendar-template.md`.
5. Hand new content ideas to `07-Daily-Brief` ("LinkedIn content
   ideas"), and any content-driven product or consulting leads to
   `03-Product-Manager` / `08-Revenue-Hunter`.

## Success Metrics

- Every published piece traceable to one of the five objectives, no
  exceptions
- Leads attributable to specific content pieces
- Newsletter subscriber growth month over month
- Organic search traffic growth to Insights and Resources pages
- Time from trigger (a regulation publishing) to published content
